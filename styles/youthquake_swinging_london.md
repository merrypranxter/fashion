# Youthquake / Swinging London

**Style ID:** `youthquake_swinging_london`  
**Active Window:** 1965–1968  
**Origin:** London, UK  
**Decade(s):** 1960s

## Overview

The mass-market amplification of mod energy. Vogue editor Diana Vreeland coined 'Youthquake' to describe the youth-driven shift in fashion power. The look is defined by disposable novelty — paper dresses, micro-minis, synthetic fabrics — and Pop Art graphic sensibility as fast fashion before the term existed.

**Cultural context:** Teen market as dominant consumer force; magazine-driven trend acceleration; Swinging London as global cultural export.

**Aesthetic opposites / contrasts:** Ladylike mid-century formality (early 1960s)
