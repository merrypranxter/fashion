# Acid House / Rave

**Style ID:** `acid_house_rave`  
**Active Window:** 1988–1992  
**Origin:** Manchester / London, UK  
**Decade(s):** 1980s–90s

## Overview

The UK's Second Summer of Love (1988–1989) produced a function-first fashion aesthetic: baggy, motion-ready, bright. Warehouse and field raves demanded clothes you could dance in for hours. The smiley face became the movement's logo; bucket hats, whistles, and neon sportswear became the visual grammar.

**Cultural context:** UK acid house music scene; illegal warehouse parties; MDMA culture; anti-Thatcher alternative community.

**Aesthetic opposites / contrasts:** Power dressing; goth; new romantic
