# Skinhead (First Wave)

**Style ID:** `skinhead_first_wave`  
**Active Window:** 1968–1971  
**Origin:** London, UK  
**Decade(s):** 1960s–70s

## Overview

A working-class British subculture that emerged from the intersection of white youth culture and Jamaican diaspora influence via ska and rocksteady music. The look is utilitarian hard lines: close-cropped hair, polo shirts, straight jeans, heavy boots, braces. The style is about working-class identity pride, not political ideology (the later politicization came later).

**Cultural context:** Working-class identity in post-war Britain; Jamaican diaspora in UK cities; ska music scene.

**Aesthetic opposites / contrasts:** Hippie bohemia; preppy collegiate
