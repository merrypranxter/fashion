# Japanese Avant-Garde / Deconstruction

**Style ID:** `japanese_avant_garde_deconstruction`  
**Active Window:** 1981–1999  
**Origin:** Tokyo, Japan / Paris (runway)  
**Decade(s):** 1980s–90s

## Overview

The 1981 Paris runway debut of Rei Kawakubo (Comme des Garçons) and Yohji Yamamoto disrupted Western shape conventions. Black palette, asymmetric construction, oversized volumes, deliberately unfinished or destroyed surfaces. The look challenges the Western body-positive display convention — clothing as question rather than decoration.

**Cultural context:** Japanese fashion industry's global breakthrough; post-WWII cultural confidence; philosophy of wabi-sabi applied to fashion.

**Aesthetic opposites / contrasts:** Power dressing; disco glamour; aerobics body-con
