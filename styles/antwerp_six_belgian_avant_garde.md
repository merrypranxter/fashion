# Antwerp Six / Belgian Avant-Garde

**Style ID:** `antwerp_six_belgian_avant_garde`  
**Active Window:** 1986–1999  
**Origin:** Antwerp, Belgium  
**Decade(s):** 1980s–90s

## Overview

Six graduates of Antwerp's Royal Academy drove a van of clothes to London Fashion Week in 1986 and changed fashion. Ann Demeulemeester, Dries Van Noten, Martin Margiela, Walter Van Beirendonck, Dirk Bikkembergs, and Dirk Van Saene brought concept-driven, art-informed fashion that extended and complicated the Japanese deconstruction project. Margiela's work in particular becomes foundational for the 1990s 'deconstruction' aesthetic.

**Cultural context:** European art school tradition; Belgian cultural scene; continuation of Japanese avant-garde disruption.

**Aesthetic opposites / contrasts:** Power dressing; mass-market trend fashion
