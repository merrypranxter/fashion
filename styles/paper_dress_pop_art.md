# Paper Dress / Pop Novelty

**Style ID:** `paper_dress_pop_art`  
**Active Window:** 1966–1967  
**Origin:** London, UK / New York, USA  
**Decade(s):** 1960s

## Overview

A very short-lived experiment in disposable clothing. Paper and cellulose-blend dresses printed with Pop Art graphics — soup cans, Op Art patterns, news imagery — were sold as fashion objects. The look is 2D: the dress as poster, not garment. The V&A documents these as objects of museum interest rather than functional clothing.

**Cultural context:** Peak Pop Art; consumer culture satire; disposability as novelty rather than problem.

**Aesthetic opposites / contrasts:** All handcrafted or durable styles
