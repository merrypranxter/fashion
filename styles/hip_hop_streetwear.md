# Hip-Hop Streetwear

**Style ID:** `hip_hop_streetwear`  
**Active Window:** 1979–1999  
**Origin:** New York City (Bronx/Brooklyn), USA  
**Decade(s):** 1980s–90s

## Overview

Emerging from the Bronx block party culture, hip-hop style developed its own language of identity, neighborhood belonging, and aspirational success. The look evolved across two decades: from Run DMC's Adidas tracksuits and unlaced sneakers (early 80s) through gold chains and basketball jerseys to the luxury-brand recontextualization of the late 90s. The NMAAHC and FIT's hip-hop exhibition are the key institutional anchors.

**Cultural context:** South Bronx block party culture; crack epidemic era economics; Black and Latino youth identity; early MTV hip-hop visibility.

**Aesthetic opposites / contrasts:** Preppy/Ivy; minimalism 90s
