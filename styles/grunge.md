# Grunge

**Style ID:** `grunge`  
**Active Window:** 1986–1994  
**Origin:** Seattle, USA  
**Decade(s):** 1990s

## Overview

The Pacific Northwest scene produced an anti-polish aesthetic of layered thrift-store clothing, flannel, ripped jeans, and combat boots. The look was not designed — it was assembled from necessity and scene identity. When Nirvana's Nevermind went mainstream in 1991, the style went with it. Marc Jacobs' 1993 Perry Ellis collection was the moment couture attempted to translate grunge — and the attempt was historically significant (he was fired).

**Cultural context:** Seattle music scene; Pacific Northwest class identity; anti-glamour as political stance.

**Aesthetic opposites / contrasts:** Minimalism 90s; power dressing; disco
