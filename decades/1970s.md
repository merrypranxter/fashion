# The 1970s: Wide Spectrum

## Decade Overview

The 1970s resist reduction. Unlike the 1960s (which had a clear mid-to-late arc) or the 1980s (which had a dominant mainstream aesthetic), the 1970s are genuinely plural: multiple major styles coexisted without a clear hierarchy.

The macro sequence: hippie continuation into prairie/boho in the early years, glam rock's theatrical gender play in the early-to-mid decade, disco's streamlined nightlife glamour expanding through the mid-to-late decade, and punk's deliberate destruction/DIY arriving late decade as counter-system.

## Key Palette Fork

**Early 70s (hippie continuation):** Earth tones, suede browns, paisley, psychedelic  
**Mid 70s (glam):** Metallics, electric brights, black, glitter  
**Mid-late 70s (disco):** Metallic, black, white, jewel tones, sequin sheen  
**Late 70s (punk):** Black, red, safety-pin silver, tartan

---

## Decade Tensions

The 1970s are especially useful for **opposition prompts**:

| Pole A | Pole B |
|--------|--------|
| Disco glam (desire + visibility) | Punk (anti-glamour + destruction) |
| Glam rock (theatrical androgyny) | Working-class utility |
| Hippie continuation (folk craft) | Synthetic nightclub polish |

---

## Decade Inflection Points

- **1971**: Glam rock emerges with Bowie's Ziggy Stardust persona
- **1974**: Disco expands; Studio 54 becomes global reference point
- **1976**: Punk formally codifies its visual system at Westwood/McLaren's SEX/Seditionaries shop
- **1978**: Punk diffuses; post-punk and new wave absorb punk energy into art-school personal style
- **1979**: Two-Tone emerges in Coventry as racially mixed ska revival
