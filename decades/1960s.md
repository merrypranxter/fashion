# The 1960s: Youth, Modernity, and Counterculture

## Decade Overview

The 1960s compress three major aesthetic phases into one decade: the continuation of mid-century ladylike formality in the early years, the explosion of youth-driven mod and Space Age modernism in the mid-decade, and the emergence of hippie eclecticism by the late 1960s.

The decade is also marked by synthetic materials experiments and disposable fashion novelty — paper dresses, PVC, and plastic accessories — that would influence later thinking about fashion and consumption.

A useful prompt fork for the decade: **mid-60s vs late-60s** gives you two very different visual languages that share a decade but almost nothing else.

## Key Palette Fork

**Mid-60s (mod / Space Age):** Black/white blocks, primary brights, optical graphic, silver, white  
**Late-60s (hippie):** Earth tones, paisley, psychedelic brights, suede browns, patchwork

---

## Material Fork

**Mid-60s:** Wool jersey, synthetics, PVC/vinyl, cellulose (paper), crisp cotton  
**Late-60s:** Suede, denim, crochet, patchwork, beads, natural fibers, secondhand

---

## Decade Inflection Points

- **1964**: Mod accelerates; hemlines genuinely start rising (contra "miniskirt in 1960" myth)
- **1966**: Hemlines reach true mini scale; Youthquake framing peaks
- **1967**: Paper/Pop novelty and hippie mixing both surge simultaneously — the great fork
- **1968**: Skinhead first wave hardens; hippie continues diffusing internationally
