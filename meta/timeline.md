# Style Movement Timeline: 1960–2000

Key inflection points and style pivots across the era. Useful for year-accurate prompt generation.

## Macro Pattern

The era oscillates between **clean/controlled** and **chaotic/DIY** across four decades:

- **1960s**: Geometric mod modernism → hippie eclecticism (clean to chaotic)
- **1970s**: Wide-spectrum decade — prairie/boho, glam, disco, punk (all at once)
- **1980s**: Power and scale → "anti-fashion" lineages (Japanese avant-garde, club culture)
- **1990s**: Minimalism and anti-excess vs. grunge and streetwear (two anti-glamours)

## Decade Overviews

### 1960s: Youth and Modernity
Three big looks compressed into one decade:
1. Continuation of ladylike mid-century formality (early 60s beatnik couture)
2. Youth-driven mod/Space Age modernism (1964–1968)
3. Late-sixties hippie eclecticism (1967–1969+)

Key fork: **PVC/paper vs suede/patchwork/kaftan** (mid vs late decade)

### 1970s: Wide Spectrum
The decade resists reduction — all of these coexisted:
- Prairie/bohemian continuation of hippie influence
- Glam rock's theatrical gender play
- Disco's streamlined nightlife glamour
- Punk's deliberate destruction/DIY

### 1980s: Power and Anti-Fashion
Two parallel tracks:
- **Mainstream**: Padded shoulders, power dressing, bold color, body-consciousness
- **Underground**: Japanese avant-garde, New Romantic club culture, acid house precursors

"Power dressing" is an explicit social technology — Museum interpretation ties it to women navigating high-powered workplaces.

### 1990s: Split Screen
Two opposing anti-glamours dominated:
- **Minimalist luxury**: reaction to 80s excess, intensified by recession
- **Grunge**: opposing anti-glamour, rooted in Pacific Northwest scene layering

Subcultures remained highly influential: Britpop revived mod codes, rave/clubwear remixed retro sportswear, hip-hop aesthetics appeared in mainstream TV.
